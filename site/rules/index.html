Editorial rules.

The texture of the brand. Not nice-to-haves — the constitution CyberFuturo Index operates under, whether or not anyone is reading.

Voice

  • Data-first, prose-second. Every brief leads with a number, not an anecdote.
  • Concrete, not speculative. "Could," "might," "is expected to" get replaced with "is," "reached," "has been."
  • Honest about limits. Every brief names what the number does not mean — usually as its own section.
  • Short paragraphs. Readers skim; skim-friendly is first-class.
  • No growth-hack emojis, no clickbait subject lines, no "you won't believe" phrasing.

The rules

  1. BR-01No forecasts. Every published claim is a measurement or a description of one.
  2. BR-02No LLM-generated numbers. Numbers come from deterministic pipelines only.
  3. BR-03Methodology before publication. An index cannot be published before its methodology document is written, reviewed, and tagged.
  4. BR-04Every number is traceable. A reader can follow brief → index → methodology → source in at most 3 clicks.
  5. BR-05Corrections appear in the next brief. Methodology errors are never silently edited out of history.
  6. BR-06One sponsor per brief. Never two, never three, never programmatic.
  7. BR-07Sponsors never appear above the fold. Editorial always comes first.
  8. BR-08Editorial independence is absolute. A sponsor cannot review, suggest, or influence editorial content beyond their own display copy.
  9. BR-09AI assistance is disclosed. Every brief that used LLM assistance for editorial prose says so, visibly, in the brief body.
  10. BR-10Subscriber data is never shared. Email addresses are not sold, swapped, or handed to sponsors — even aggregate segments. See privacy policy.
  11. BR-11Every index has a kill criterion. If an index stops being useful or reliable, it is deprecated, not silently rotted.
  12. BR-12Git history is the audit trail. Past brief content and past dataset values remain accessible. No destructive history rewrites.

What a brief never contains

  • Predictions
  • Investment advice
  • "Top 10" / "5 things to watch" style listicles
  • Political opinion
  • Personal speculation dressed as analysis
  • LLM-generated numbers, charts, tables, or metadata
  • Unattributed numbers
  • Engagement-bait CTAs
